Step 0: Understand ssh-key

First, I believe it's critical to know ... I mean really understand how ssh-keys work. You can skip this step if you understand the ssh-key concept. Also, if you know how to create ssh-keys and authenticate them on github, go directly to step 3.

ssh is a remote access method. So, a ssh-key is a key to remotely access something. Having this key let's the remote computer or server know that it is you who is accessing it.

Step 1: Generate ssh-key

First, we need to create a ssh-key. Follow Steps 1 to 4 from this link: "Generating a new SSH key". Note that if you are working on your personal computer, you just need to press "enter" after step 2 in the link.

Let me explain what this step does. You are creating a ssh-key and when you are done creating your key, you can check that it's made in "~/.ssh" repository.

You can see that you have two files created: "ed25519" and "ed25519.pub". You can change their names however you want, e.g. as shown below.
